# Strategy — ep001: The Pilot

## Hook angle

The enterprise did not fail to adopt AI. It adopted the process of adopting AI. Eighteen months in, the pilot is the product.

## Why this works

The joke is structural, not verbal — there's no punchline, only escalation. Each governance layer is presented as a reasonable response to the previous one. The audience recognizes the machine before we name it. This is the Gary Larson move: the caption describes exactly what you're looking at, and that's why it's funny. We never editorialize. The committee is doing its job.

## Format recommendation

**Card + broll.** Empty conference rooms and overhead office shots amplify the deadpan. The spaces look exactly like where this decision would be made. Broll carries scenes 2 and 4; cards open and close.

## Retention structure

1. **Hook (0–4 s):** The stat. Not framed as a problem — stated as a finding. "Ninety percent of enterprise AI initiatives are in pilot." Full stop.
2. **Body (4–45 s):** Three escalating beats. Beat one: the initiative itself — what it was supposed to do, how long it's been running. Beat two: the governance layer — the committee, the framework, the approval process for the approval process. Beat three: the meeting — not to resolve anything, but to schedule a follow-up.
3. **Button (45–65 s):** The initiative remains in pilot. The committee has been renewed for another quarter. Approved. LGTM.

## Scene count and target duration

- Recommended scene count: 7 scenes
- Target duration: 60–68 seconds
- Rationale: Four VO scenes at ~8–10 s each plus two broll holds and a closing card lands cleanly in the 60 s short-form window.

## B-roll search terms

- empty conference room long table overhead fluorescent light
- open plan office empty desks no people
- whiteboard covered in sticky notes no one nearby
- server room blinking lights no people
- close up laptop screen dark office

## Brand voice notes for script agent

1. Every sentence should sound like it was excerpted from an internal memo. Passive voice where it adds institutional weight: "The initiative was approved." "The timeline has been adjusted."
2. Never name the company, the AI tool, or the use case. This is every enterprise, not one enterprise.
3. The closing line must land dry and final — no rising intonation implied, no ellipsis energy. It ends. That's the joke.
