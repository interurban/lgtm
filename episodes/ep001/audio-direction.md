# Audio Direction — ep001: The Pilot

## Music direction

**Mood:** flat, institutional, faintly oppressive, patient
**Genre / reference:** sparse ambient electronic — think William Basinski or early Eno without the warmth. Corporate hold music that has been running for eighteen months and shows no signs of stopping.
**Tempo:** very slow, or effectively beatless — no discernible pulse
**Instrumentation:** long sine-wave tones, faint room tone, maybe a distant low hum. No percussion. No melody. If there is a piano, it plays one note every twelve seconds and means nothing.
**What to avoid:** anything with a beat, a progression, a build, or a resolution. No uplifting major-key pads. No cinematic strings. Nothing that implies something is about to happen. Nothing is about to happen.

## Energy arc

The music enters at the same volume it will maintain for the entire episode — there is no swell, no arc, no drama. It is simply present, like background noise in an office where the air conditioning has been broken for three weeks and nobody has filed a ticket because the ticketing system is also in pilot. At s07 (the silent button card), the music does not swell to compensate for the silence — it continues unchanged, or cuts entirely to true silence. True silence is the stronger choice. Decide based on what the VO's last line needs to land.

## Fade

**Fade starts:** 42s (just as s07 begins)
**Fade duration:** 4s
**Alternative:** hard cut to silence at 41.5s for maximum deadpan. Recommended.

## SFX cues

No SFX recommended for this episode. The joke does not need punctuation. Silence serves the tone.

## Music bed volume

**Recommended:** 0.08 — this is a dense VO episode with six spoken scenes. The music should be barely perceptible; more felt than heard. If in doubt, go lower.
**Hard ceiling:** 0.15 (never exceed, per pipeline rules)

## Sourcing notes

Search terms that will surface the right vibe:
- Free Music Archive: "ambient drone" / "minimal ambient" / "dark ambient"
- Pixabay Music: "ambient corporate" / "minimal background" / "corporate ambient drone"
- YouTube Audio Library: "ambient" filtered by "dark" or "atmospheric"

Avoid anything tagged "motivational," "inspiring," "upbeat," or "cinematic." If the track sounds like it belongs in a product demo, it does not belong here.

The ideal track sounds like the music that plays in the lobby of a building where a committee is meeting to discuss a committee.
