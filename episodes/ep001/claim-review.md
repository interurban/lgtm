## s01 — APPROXIMATE

**Claim:** "Ninety percent of enterprise AI initiatives are currently in pilot."
**Sources found:**
- SoftwareSeni (citing IDC/industry data): "Why 88 to 95 Percent of Enterprise AI Pilots Never Reach Production"
- McKinsey: "Nearly two-thirds of organisations remain stuck in pilot mode, unable to scale across the enterprise"
- Digital Applied (March 2026): "78% of enterprises have AI agent pilots, but only 14% reach production scale" — implies 86% not scaling
- Infor AI Adoption Impact Index (April 2026): "more than half of businesses struggle to scale AI"

**Verdict:** APPROXIMATE. The 90% figure falls within a published range (88–95%) and is the most quotable single number from that range. However, the most current sourced figure for "pilots that never reach production" is the 88–95% range; the most recent scaling-gap figure is 86% (78% piloting, 14% reaching production). 

**Recommendation:** "Ninety percent" is defensible as a round number within the published range. For maximum accuracy, consider changing to:
- "Eighty-eight to ninety-five percent" — accurate, but kills the rhythm
- "Fewer than one in seven enterprise AI pilots reach production" — sourced directly from the 78%/14% data, more vivid
- Keep "ninety percent" as-is — within range, cleaner for VO

**Decision for creative director:** Keep "ninety percent." It is within the published range, and the specific figure is less important than the structural truth the episode is illustrating. Flag in show notes if needed.
