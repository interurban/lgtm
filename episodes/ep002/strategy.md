# Strategy: OpenClaw in the Enterprise

### Hook angle

An enterprise buys an AI coding assistant to go faster — then spends nine months building a governance apparatus so sophisticated that no one has actually used it yet.

### Why this works

The gap between the promise ("40% productivity gain") and the reality (zero output, infinite committees) is the joke. No irony needed — the situation is self-indicting. This is pure Mike Judge: bureaucracy as its own reward, processes that exist to govern other processes. The prompt engineer who cannot yet prompt is the Gary Larson cow who doesn't know what a slaughterhouse is.

### Format recommendation

**Card + kinetic + mockup + broll** — mixed scene types to maximize kinetic energy. Kinetic numbers land the zero/cost stats; JIRA and Slack mockups show the machinery of inaction; broll punctuates the absurdity.

### Retention structure

1. **Hook (0–5 s):** Open with the corporate promise — then immediately undercut it with "nine months ago." Specific stat (40%) makes it feel real before the rug pull.
2. **Body:** Escalate through the governance stack — committee meetings (zero approvals), JIRA ticket governing the governance of the tool, Slack thread where the form 404s, prompt engineer still pending access.
3. **Callback / button:** Land on a cost kinetic ($2.4M in governance spend) and let that sit before the LGTM button. The silence after the number is the punchline.

### Scene count and target duration

- Recommended scene count: 10 scenes
- Target duration: 29–32 seconds
- Rationale: two kinetic punches at either end of the body, two mockups for depth, broll for the committee meeting beat.

### B-roll search terms

- boring office meeting man asleep
- empty conference room fluorescent
- office worker staring screen waiting
- typing keyboard frustrated

### Brand voice notes for script agent

- Do not say "unfortunately" or "ironically" — the facts speak for themselves
- The prompt engineer line should be deadpan and specific: what they are, not what they can't do yet
- Numbers beat adjectives: "nine months" not "nearly a year," "forty percent" not "significant gains"
- CLAIM: $2.4M governance spend — fictional/plausible, flag for claim-review if episode goes to broadcast
