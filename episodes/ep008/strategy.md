# Strategy — ep008: Dilbert's Hot Take on OpenClaw

### Hook angle

The enterprise has finally shipped an assistant named like a hazard label, and the only adult in the room is still wearing a white shirt and a curved tie.

### Why this works

LGTM runs on bureaucratic inevitability, not punchlines. OpenClaw-style autonomy maps cleanly onto calendar blocks, ticket queues, and permission dialogs. The Mike Judge layer is that every safeguard is another meeting.

### Format recommendation

**Card + broll** — cards carry the thesis; stock footage keeps the world grounded in beige carpet and fluorescent calm.

### Retention structure

1. **Hook (0–5 s):** Name the thing and the tone. The audience should know this is about autonomy meeting policy in one beat.
2. **Body:** Escalate from announcement to permissions to incident ticket to calendar. Let the metadata do the comedy.
3. **Callback / button:** Close on merge language. Silence after the brand line.

### Scene count and target duration

- Recommended scene count: 10 scenes
- Target duration: 38–42 seconds
- Rationale: Enough room for two mockups, one calendar, one kinetic pair, and one broll beat without dragging.

### B-roll search terms (if applicable)

empty office cubicles fluorescent overhead wide shot
corporate hallway security camera angle static
conference room table empty chairs overhead

### Brand voice notes for script agent

- Third-person deadpan narrator. No winking at OpenClaw or Dilbert by name in every line.
- Two sentences max per scene. Prefer one.
- Do not invent survey statistics; if you use a number, keep it clearly fictional or label it as internal policy text in-dialogue, not as a real-world fact.
